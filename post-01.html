What are primates?

Primates are a taxonomic order of eutherian mammals. They arose 85–55 million years ago first from small terrestrial mammals, which adapted to living in the trees of tropical forests: many primate characteristics represent adaptations to life in this challenging environment, including large brains, visual acuity, color vision, a shoulder girdle allowing a large degree of movement in the shoulder joint, and dextrous hands. Primates range in size from Madame Berthe's mouse lemur, which weighs 30 g, to the eastern gorilla, weighing over 200 kg.

There are 376–522 species of living primates (depending on which classification is used.) New primate species continue to be discovered: over 25 species were described in the 2000s, 36 in the 2010s, and three in the 2020s!
